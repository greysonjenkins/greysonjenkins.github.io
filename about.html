About

I graduated from Pepperdine University in 2023 with a dual degree in Computer Science and Philosophy and a minor in Great Books. My academic work includes co‐authoring research on computer‐vision models for computer‐assisted medical diagnostics. "Weakly-Supervised Convolutional Neural Networks for Vessel Segmentation in Cerebral Angiography" Professionally, I have worked as a Data Science Intern at a leading healthcare technology company, applying ML methods to identify new client leads.

Additionally, at Pepperdine, I was an undergraduate research fellow at the Keck Institute for Data Science Keck Institute for Data Science and a regular volunteer at Pepperdine's annual Human-Centered AI conference. Visit the conference website

I differentiate myself from other thinkers through my formal education in computer science, philosophy, and studying the Great Books. At Pepperdine, I was trained in the analytic tradition, with philosophy classes emphasizing symbolic logic and computer science classes emphasizing discrete mathematics and computation theory. Consequently, where most developers and data scientists advertise their experience at the intersection of multiple fields, I stand out due to my training in the union of multiple fields.
